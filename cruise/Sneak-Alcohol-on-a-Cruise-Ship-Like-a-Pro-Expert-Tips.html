Are there any risks involved with trying to sneak alcohol on a cruise ship?

  • Getting caught and removed from the ship: Cruise lines take considerable measures to prevent people from sneaking alcohol on board and those caught trying can be removed from the ship.
  • Paying fines: Those caught attempting to bring alcohol on board can face financial consequences and have to pay a fine which can cost up to hundreds of dollars.
  • Risk of getting confiscated: Any alcohol brought on board can be confiscated and will not be returned to the individual once confiscated.
  • Risk of getting arrested: In some countries, bringing alcohol on board a cruise ship some cruises are not allowed and those caught could be arrested.



How can you ensure that you don't get caught when sneaking alcohol on a cruise ship?

  • Be discreet: Wear baggy clothes to conceal any items you bring and don’t draw too much attention.
  • Research your cruise line’s policy on alcohol: Different cruise lines will have different policies that you need to be aware of.
  • Stick to smaller bottles: Larger bottles are more likely to get noticed.
  • Sneak alcohol onto the ship in another container: Put your alcohol into a water or soda bottle.
  • Hide it in your luggage: Pack your alcohol at the bottom of your bags and hide it under other items.
  • Skip heavy-duty drinks: Avoid bringing any alcohol that is more likely to be caught, spirits may result in harsher consequences if caught when wines can receive more leniency.
  • Don’t brag about your smuggling skills: Be as discreet as possible and don’t make it obvious that you are trying to sneak alcohol onto the ship.
  • Don’t be too greedy: Don’t try to bring too much. Stow away what you need and leave the rest behind.
