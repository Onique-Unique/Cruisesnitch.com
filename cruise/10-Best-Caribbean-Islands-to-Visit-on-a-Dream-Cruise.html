10 Best Caribbean Islands to Visit in 2023 for a Dream Cruise

A dream Caribbean cruise in 2023 awaits you with breathtaking islands to explore. The Caribbean is home to some of the world's most stunning and diverse landscapes, and a cruise is the perfect way to experience them all.

But with so many islands to choose from, it can be overwhelming to plan your itinerary. That's why we've put together a list of the 10 best Caribbean islands to visit in 2023 for a dream cruise, complete with must-see attractions and local insights to help you make the most of your trip.

  • Jamaica: With its beautiful white sand beaches, picturesque rainforests, and vibrant culture, Jamaica is an ideal Caribbean destination for a dream cruise experience.
  • Turks and Caicos: Boasting some of the Caribbean's most stunning beaches and crystal clear waters, Turks and Caicos is the perfect place to soak up the sun and frolic in the surf.
  • Grand Cayman: Grand Cayman's idyllic coastal setting, rich history, and Stingray City make it a must-visit Caribbean destination.
  • Aruba: Sparkling white sand beaches and non-stop party atmosphere make Aruba an excellent destination for a dream cruise.
  • The Dominican Republic: From the historic city of Santo Domingo to the breathtaking beaches of Punta Cana, the Dominican Republic offers something for everyone.
  • Puerto Rico: With its lush jungles, vibrant cities, and beautiful beaches, Puerto Rico has much to offer travelers in search of a Caribbean dream cruise experience.
  • St. Maarten: St. Maarten is a Caribbean paradise, offering picturesque beaches, tropical activities, and plenty of shopping and dining options.
  • Antigua and Barbuda: With its stunningly blue waters, sandy beaches, and luxury resorts, Antigua and Barbuda make for an unforgettable Caribbean dream cruise experience.
  • The Bahamas: The Bahamas offers crystal clear waters perfect for swimming and snorkeling, as well as plenty of fun activities for the whole family.
  • Virgin Islands: A Caribbean dream cruise would not be complete without a visit to the breathtaking Virgin Islands, with its stunning beaches and laid-back vibes.




What are the best activities to do on a Caribbean island during a cruise stopover/ overnight?

  • Relax on the beaches: Take in the crystal blue waters and enjoy some of the world's most beautiful beaches.
  • Visit a rum factory: Sample some of the best rum in the Caribbean while learning more about the famous beverage.
  • Take a snorkeling tour: Make some amazing memories while seeing the tropical fish and other unique sea creatures in the area.
  • Enjoy water sports: Paddle boarding, parasailing and kayaking are just a few of the fun activities you can enjoy on the island.
  • Shop at local markets: Get some one-of-a-kind souvenirs and learn more about the traditional culture and foods of the Caribbean
  • Watch a sunset: The Caribbean sunsets are some of the most breathtaking in the world and definitely something to witness while in the area.
  • Party it up: The islands are known for having some of the best nightclubs and bars in the world, so dance the night away and create some fun memories.
  • Watch a steel drum band: Immerse yourself in the Caribbean music scene and feel the rhythms come alive as you watch local bands playing drums.



What are the best local dishes to try on each of the 10 recommended Caribbean islands?

  • Bermuda - Loquat Pie: This sweet local classic is made with a flaky, buttery crust and filled with mashed loquats, a tangy fruit that is native to the island.
  • Barbados - Flying Fish: A staple of the Barbadian cuisine, this delicacy is typically served curried or stewed in either a brown sugar or a tamarind sauce.
  • The Bahamas - Conch Fritters: Fried conch meat mixed with a crunchy coating of flour, onion, and pepper is an absolute must-have.
  • Cuba - Ropa Vieja: This Cuban classic is made with beef that's shredded, simmered in tomato-based sauce and served with rice, beans and plantains.
  • Dominican Republic - Sancocho: This traditional stew is a blend of seven fresh ingredients and spices. It's simmered slowly until the flavors are combined and the dish is full and flavorful.
  • Jamaica - Jerk Chicken: Slow-cooked chicken is rubbed with various spices that gives it the signature jerk flavor. It's usually served with savory sides like rice and peas and festival, a fried dumpling.
  • Puerto Rico - Mofongo: Mashed fried plantains are mixed with garlic, olive oil, and chicharron (fried pork cracklings) and then topped with a favorite protein such as chicken, beef, shrimp or lobster.
  • Haiti - Griot: Pork chunks are marinated with citrus juice, aromatic herbs and spices, and served with a side of rice and beans.
  • Virgin Islands - Tostones: Fried and slightly flattened green plantains are served with traditional accompaniments like mayoketchup and ketchup-mayo.
  • Trinidad & Tobago - Doubles: This beloved street food is made with two pieces of fried flatbread called barra and stuffed with spicy curried chickpeas and chutney.
