Pier Runners Beware: What to Consider Before Your Next Cruise in 2023

Planning your next cruise can be an exciting and fun experience, but it's important to be aware of potential issues that can arise, especially for pier runners. Pier runners are passengers who arrive at the port late and risk being left behind by their ship.

To avoid this stressful situation, there are several factors to consider before your next cruise in 2023, including the time of year, transportation arrangements, and travel insurance coverage.

What are the most common reasons for pier runners missing their cruise ship departure time?

  • Unfamiliarity with the port: If the runner has never been to the port before, it can take them longer to find their way around.
  • Traffic: Heavy traffic, especially around peaks times, can slow down and even stop the runner from reaching their destination on time.
  • Poor planning: Not planning ahead, failing to check with the port authority and not checking the time can be a major cause for one missing their departure time.
  • Misreading timetables/ itinerary: Misunderstanding the cruise timetables and not interpreting it correctly can lead to the runner running late for their cruise shipsâ€™ departure.
  • Poor weather conditions: Heavy rains, storms and other weather changes can slow down the driver and cause delays.

  • Medical emergencies: Passengers can be late back to the ship if they experience some form of medical or health episode ashore, if this happens - it is generally wise to contact the ship/ port agent to make them aware.
