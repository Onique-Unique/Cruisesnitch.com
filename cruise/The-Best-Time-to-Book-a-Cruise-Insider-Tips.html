The Best Time to Book a Cruise: Insider Tips for 2023

If you're planning to book a cruise in 2023, timing can be everything. Knowing the best time to book a cruise can make a significant difference in price, availability, and overall experience.

With these insider tips and tricks, you can make the most out of your vacation budget and secure the ideal itinerary for your dream getaway.

2023 has a lot to offer and it's time to start planning your cruise!

Here are the best times to book a cruise for maximum savings:

  • Early: The earlier you book, the more likely you are to secure the best deals.
  • December to March: High demand cruises tend to offer more promotions and discounts.
  • June-August: This is typically the peak season, so you'll be able to find the best deals on cruises and popular ports.
  • “Wave Season”: Cruise lines typically offer discounts or promotions during the months of January and February.
  • Last-Minute Deals: Some cruise companies offer discounts on select trips that are booked within a couple of weeks or a month before departure.
  • Last-Minute Specials: If you’re looking for a deal and want the extra flexibility of a last-minute booking, some companies offer vacation packages with discounted rates.
  • Special Offers: Keep an eye out for promotions and discounts from cruise companies.
  • Stay Loyal: Loyal customers usually get the best deals and discounts for repeat cruises.
  • Book in Advance: Cruise companies frequently offer advance booking discounts.




What are the advantages of booking a cruise early, and how can you find the best deals?

  • Save Money: By booking your cruise early, you’ll have access to early bird discounts, discounted rates, free onboard activities and even exclusive packages.
  • More Choices: The earlier you book a cruise, the more options you will have for ship, cabin and itinerary selection.
  • Flexibility: By booking early, you’re also more likely to be able to switch or cancel without paying cancellation fees.
  • Investment Protection: With an early booking, you won’t have to worry that the price of the cruise will increase or that only much pricier options will be available.
  • Find Deals: Some of the best deals are found by signing up for cruise line emails or through online travel agents. Alternatively, you may consider a negotiated cruise rate with a travel agent or ship specialist who may be able to further discount your fare.



How can you choose the right type of cabin for your needs and preferences?

  • Think about: who will be staying in the cabin with you and the amount of space you need.
  • Consider: the features that you need from the cabin, such as en-suite bathroom, windows/balcony, disabled access.
  • Prioritise: which features are most important to you, such as an en-suite bathroom or beautiful views from a window/balcony.
  • Choose the right: cabin size for your group, such as single occupancy cabins, double occupancy cabins, family size cabins.
  • Look at the ‘Fine Print’: of the cabin, such as what additional services are provided and any extra costs.
  • Read online reviews: of the cabin if any and other cabins to determine the pros and cons of each option.
  • Make a budget: and decide if it’s worth paying more for a certain cabin type.
  • Visit the cruise company’s website: or speak to a representative for more details and advice.



What are some key considerations for selecting a cruise line and itinerary that align with your travel goals and interests?

  • Look into: the onboard activities offered by the cruise line - A good cruise line should have plenty of onboard activities and entertainment that can take your vacation to the next level and keep everyone in the family entertained.
  • Review the dining options: - Is the cruise ship known for its cuisine? Does it provide a variety of food from different cultures? Are there options for specialty dining?
  • Consider your destination: - What kind of scenery and culture do you hope to experience during your trip? Consider the type of destinations that a cruise line offers when selecting your itinerary.
  • Examine the ports of call: - How many days will you be in each port? Are there any additional fees or excursions that you can add to your itinerary?
  • Read the reviews: - Check out reviews from past passengers to get an idea of the quality of the cruise line, the service onboard and their experiences at the destinations.
  • Compare the cost: - Look into different cruise lines, and make sure to read the fine print so you know what is included in the base cost and any extra costs you may incur.
