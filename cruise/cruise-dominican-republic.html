  • Research the ports of call: Get to know the ports of call in the Dominican Republic in advance. Find out the typical activities and sights each has to offer. Know which ones have childcare or family-friendly activities.
  • Book an All-Inclusive Cruise: Enlist the services of a travel agent to book an all-inclusive cruise to the Dominican Republic. Make sure to look for competitively-priced packages that include food, drinks and shore excursions.
  • Create an Itinerary: Plan your stops ahead of time by researching places to go and creating an itinerary. Consider renting a car, taking public transport or booking a tour when you arrive in each port.
  • Know the Weather: Dominica has a tropical climate and can be humid during the summer months. If you're looking to visit during that time, bring plenty of sunscreen.
  • Plan Activities & Shore Excursions: Prepare a list of activities and shore excursions that match your interests. Entertainment and sightseeing opportunities abound in the Dominican Republic. Be sure to check out the local culture and take in historical sites.
  • Pack Smart: Make sure to pack light and smart. You'll want to bring clothing for the beach and evening activities including resort wear, casual and evening clothing, swimwear and a few sundresses for women.
  • Get the Necessary Documents: Check the requirements for a visa and passport with your cruise line well in advance. Make sure to have those ready before you set sail.
  • Budget Accordingly: Think ahead and decide how much you're willing to spend during your trip. There may be hidden costs such as port taxes, meal gratuities and shore excursions so plan your budget accordingly.
  • Get Travel Insurance: Protect your trip by investing in a comprehensive travel insurance policy that protects you financially should an emergency arise.
