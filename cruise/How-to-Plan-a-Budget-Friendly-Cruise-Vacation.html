How to Plan a Budget-Friendly Cruise Vacation in 2023

Dreaming of a cruise vacation in 2023 but worried about breaking the bank? Planning a budget-friendly cruise can seem unrealistic, but with the right strategy and tips, it's definitely doable.

From choosing the right time to book to finding affordable excursions, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know to plan a budget-friendly cruise vacation in 2023 without sacrificing the fun and adventure that cruising offers.

What are some insider tips for finding the best deals on cruise vacations?

  • Book Early: Cruise lines typically offer discounts and promotions to those who book their vacations early.
  • Consider Cruises During Shoulder Season: Most cruise lines will offer reduced prices during the period between peak and off-peak travel.
  • Sign Up for Cruise Lines' Loyalty Programs: Cruise lines will offer exclusive deals and discounts to those who join their loyalty programs.
  • Look for Last-Minute Offers: Some cruise lines will offer last-minute deals to fill empty cabins before the ship departs.
  • Comparison-Shop: Cruise fare prices can vary wildly between different travel agencies and cruise lines, so it's always important to compare rates from multiple sources.
  • Sign Up for Deals and Newsletters: Many cruise lines offer newsletters or deals that can be sent to your email. It pays to be signed up, so you can grab those deals as soon as they become available.
  • Try Bidding Sites: Priceline and Hotwire both offer bidding services for cruises, which can be a great way to save money if you're willing to take a chance.
  • Take Advantage of Group Rates: Consider booking a trip with a group of friends or family, as cruise lines often offer discounts for larger bookings.



How can you save money on board without missing out on the amenities and experiences?

  • Prioritize your spending: Think about which amenities, activities, experiences, or services are the most important and spend your money accordingly.
  • Explore discounts and bundle deals: Research online or ask at the front desk if any discounts or bundles are available. Alaska Airlines, for example, offers a variety of promotion packages.
  • Donâ€™t be afraid to ask: If you want to do something, ask if there are affordable options! There are often ways to get discounted rates if you inquire politely.
  • Take advantage of free amenities: On the cruise ship and throughout the destination, ask what activities are free or discounted.
  • Seek out local attractions and dining: Look for off-peak discounts or attractions that are open late. Try to avoid overly touristy spots for your meals and opt for smaller, local restaurants for a unique experience at a lower cost.
  • Cut extras: You don't need to sign up for a bunch of features you don't really need. Instead, focus on essential amenities.
  • Stay in: Enjoy the beautiful ship with a night in and enjoy a movie, play a game, or read a book that can be picked up from the library!
  • Rely on public transit: Save money on cabs and rental cars by opting to use public transit.
  • Do your own tours: Instead of taking a bus or guided tour, explore the area on foot or take public transit.
  • Buy tickets in bulk: If you plan to do multiple activities that require tickets, ask if you can purchase them in bulk at a discounted rate!

  • Bring a guidebook: or look for online resources for each of the ports of call before you set sail. This can help you plan activities and determine what attractions are available for free or for a low cost.
  • Rent: bicycles or motorbikes at the port of call, and explore the area on your own.
  • Take part: in a walking tour of the area. Many ports of call offer free walking tours, or you can find one independently.
  • Learn to bargain: with the locals and barter for items and experiences.
  • Chat with: cruise ship staff and ask where locals like to go. Some restaurants, beaches, cultural centers and tourist attractions are unknown to most tourists, but often the locals know the best spots in town.
  • Find a local: or shared shuttle to explore the area, or to a specific attraction nearby.
  • Visit a museum or art gallery: Most museums will have a reduced entry fee, or offer some free days throughout the year.
  • Attend a cultural event: Many ports of call will have local shows, festivals or concerts that you can take part in for an inexpensive fee or for free.
