Exploring the Mediterranean: The Best Ports to Visit on a Cruise in 2023

Cruising the Mediterranean is a dream for many travelers, and in 2023, it's more accessible than ever. With so many ports to choose from, it can be overwhelming to decide which ones to visit.

That's why we've compiled a list of the best ports to explore on a Mediterranean cruise in 2023. From stunning architecture to mouth-watering cuisine, this guide will help you plan the ultimate Mediterranean adventure.

  • Istanbul, Turkey: Nestled between two continents, Istanbul seamlessly blends an impressive blend of both European and Asian culture. Take a Bosphorus Strait scenic cruise, hop between grand mosques and on-the-water galley venues, or shop for some of the city's destinations for top designers and local artisans.
  • Athens, Greece: Visit the birthplace of democracy, philosophy and theatre at the legendary Acropolis, take in the breath-taking Parthenon and get lost in the winding streets of Monastiraki. Take time for a visit to the National Archaeological Museum as well, for one of the most comprehensive collections of ancient artifacts in the world.
  • Valletta, Malta: All the impressive structures, mixed with the town's rich history, makes Valletta the perfect Mediterranean cruise stop. From the majestic 16th-century citadel to the stunning Baroque and Renaissance architecture, Valletta is a picturesque and awe-inspiring city.
  • Kusadasi, Turkey: Bustling and beautiful, Kusadasi is your go-to destination for brilliant bazaars, stunning beaches and attractions such as Miletus, House of the Virgin Mary and Pamukkale.
  • Naples, Italy: A classic Italian port, Naples, is the perfect place to indulge in Italian cuisine, cruise around the beautiful Bay of Naples and take in the historic sights in Naples itself.
  • Piraeus, Greece: Offering the perfect opportunity to take a day-trip to the iconic city of Athens, the port of Piraeus is renowned for its cafes, shopping arcades, vibrant nightlife and friendly locals.
  • Cagliari, Italy: Home to some of Sardinia's best-preserved beaches, the ancient and bustling port of Cagliari caters to beach-lovers alike, with a taste for history and maybe a spot of shopping.
  • Barcelona, Spain: Perhaps one of the most vibrant ports along the Mediterranean cruise circuit, the city of Barcelona certainly demands repeat visits with its great food, nightlife, culture and art.
  • Marseille, France: Synonymous with bohemian lifestyle, vibrant atmosphere and world-class seafood, the old port of Marseille has seen an impressive transformation.
  • Genoa, Italy: The ancient harbour town of Genoa offers a wealth of historic and cultural attractions, including impressive palaces, museums, and churches, as well as a picturesque old town and bustling port area.




What are the top cultural experiences to have while exploring the Mediterranean on a cruise?

  • Stroll Through a Mediterranean Market: Get a taste of local culture by browsing through bustling markets filled with fresh food, local specialities, and unique souvenirs.
  • Attend a Local Festival/ Events: Experience traditional and contemporary celebrations of culture and life, such as the Moorish Holiday of Andalusia or the ancient Greek Zaxaroula Festival.
  • Tour a Historical Site: Explore life from a different time and place by visiting archaeological ruins and historical monuments, such as the Parthenon in Greece or the Roman Colosseum in Italy.
  • Sail on a Traditional Boat: Take a leisurely cruise on a wooden sailboat and experience the Mediterranean Sea in its purest form.
  • Safari in Morocco: Take a drive on the desert and spot exotic wildlife while being surrounded by ancient Ksar villages and traditional trading posts.
  • Feast on Mediterranean Cuisine: Indulge in traditional dishes such as pizza, paella, moussaka, and hummus that pack in nutrients and flavor.
  • Wander Through an Ancient Village: Visit a small village or island and get an intimate glimpse of life in the Mediterranean.
