How can you prepare for an adventure-packed cruise itinerary?

  • Research the Cruise Destination & Itinerary: Make sure to learn about the places you will visit on your cruise and any activities that are offered. That way, you'll know what to expect and can plan accordingly.
  • Pack the Necessary Items: Depending on the type of cruise, you'll need to pack different items. For example, if there are watersports, you may want to bring sun protection and swimwear. If outdoor activities, you may want to bring proper hiking clothing and shoes.
  • Prepare for Unexpected Weather: Check the weather forecast for the destination, so you can pack the appropriate clothes and gear. Bring layers, so you can layer up or down depending on the temperature.
  • Get Travel Insurance: To give you peace of mind, consider getting travel insurance to cover any medical costs or other unexpected expenses.
  • Register Your Trip: If you are traveling to multiple countries on the cruise, you may need to register your trip with the Smart Traveler Enrollment Program (STEP) before you go.
  • If Required - Get Vaccinations: Depending on where you are traveling, you may need to get certain vaccinations. Check the CDC website to learn any recommended vaccinations and make sure to get them before your trip.
  • Allow Time to Adjust: Jet lag from multiple days of traveling and crossing time zones can become a factor. Make sure to factor in some downtime so your body can adjust and enjoy your cruise.
  • Contact Your Bank & Credit Card Companies: Let your bank and credit card companies know where you will be traveling and for how long so that your cards won't be blocked due to suspicious activity.
  • Backup Important Documents: Make copies or scan important travel documents, like your passports and cruise tickets, and store them in a secure online space like Google Drive. That way, even if the original documents get lost, you can access the scanned versions.
  • Pack Enough Cash: While credit cards are a convenient way to pay at most destinations, it's still useful to bring some cash. Check with the cruise line to find out what type of foreign currencies they accept and make sure to bring the necessary denominations.
