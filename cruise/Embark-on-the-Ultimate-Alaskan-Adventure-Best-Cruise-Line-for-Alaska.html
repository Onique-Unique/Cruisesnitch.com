What are the best ways to spot and photograph the incredible wildlife on an Alaskan cruise?

  • Wake up early to catch the blue light of the sunrise: In Alaska, the early morning hours are the best time to spot and photograph wildlife. The soft morning light provides an ideal backdrop for capturing stunning photos.
  • Join a guided wildlife excursion: Book excursions with experienced guides who have expert knowledge of the wildlife that regularly appears in the area. They can provide helpful tips on where to look and may even have access to some of the best spots.
  • Bring binoculars and telescopes: Binoculars are essential for wildlife spotting and photographing from the deck of a ship. Invest in a pair of powerful binoculars and a quality telescope for better results.
  • Have patience: Sometimes simply being in the right place and waiting with patience is the best way to spot and photograph wildlife. Set aside some time every day to simply sit back and wait.
  • Use a telephoto lens: A telephoto lens will enable you to capture photos at a distance without disturbing the wildlife too much. The animals won't be so intimidated by the size of your lens, making them more likely to stay in view.
  • Stay quiet: Speak softly and move slowly while out wildlife spotting to help the animals feel more at ease. This will ensure they stay in view for longer and give you more time to take amazing photos.
  • Be aware of weather conditions: Keep an eye on the weather and plan your photography sessions for days when the light is perfect and the skies are clear. The perfect conditions will deliver the most stunning and sharp images of the wildlife.
