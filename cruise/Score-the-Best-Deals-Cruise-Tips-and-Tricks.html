What additional costs should you be aware of when booking a cruise, and how can you budget accordingly to avoid unexpected expenses?

  • Gratuities: These are fees you typically pay for the staff and crew on the cruise ship and can range from $13 - $20 per person, per day.
  • Shore Excursions: If you'd like to explore different ports your cruise is visiting, you'll typically have to pay for additional excursions. Prices vary depending upon the tour, but it's a good idea to have a budget in mind before you depart.
  • Drinks & Alcohol: Most cruises charge additional fees for alcoholic beverages and specialty coffees, and some ships even have corkage fees if you want to bring your own alcohol on board.
  • Items & Souvenirs: On-board shops sell everything from toiletries to clothing, jewelry, and souvenirs, so you'll need to budget accordingly for any items you may want to buy.
  • Wi-Fi: Access to the ship's Wi-Fi usually comes with an extra charge, so factor this into your budget as well.
  • Spa Treatments: A massage or facial in the ship's spa can come with steep prices, so you may need to budget for these in advance as well.
  • Travel Insurance: Many cruise companies offer travel insurance in case of illness or injury, but you may need to purchase your own policy if you want to be covered during the course of your voyage.

By having a clear understanding of the additional costs that may be associated with a cruise, you can budget accordingly and prepare in advance to avoid any surprise charges during the course of your voyage.
