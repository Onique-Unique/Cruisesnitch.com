Ports In Miami For Cruises: Beginners Guide 2023

Miami is a premier destination for cruising enthusiasts. With its bustling port, travelers can easily access a variety of exciting destinations. However, with so many ports in Miami for cruises, it can be overwhelming to decide where to start.

In this guide, we'll explore the top ports in Miami for cruises and what you can expect from each one. Whether you're planning your first cruise or your tenth, this guide will help you navigate your way to the perfect vacation.

  • Port Miami: Offering cruises year-round, this is the largest and most popular cruise port in Miami. It has five terminals, including Celebrity Cruises, Carnival Cruise Line, Royal Caribbean International, and Norwegian Cruise Line.
  • Port Everglades: Cruise lines like Azamara, Celebrity Cruises, Carnival, Royal Caribbean International, and Holland America all depart from Port Everglades. It's located in Fort Lauderdale and offers easy access to Miami International Airport and downtown Miami.
  • Port of Palm Beach: A smaller port, located less than an hour North of Miami, it is home to Royal Caribbean's Empress of the Seas. It is also popular with smaller cruising vessels.
  • Port Canaveral: The closest port to Miami, located in Central Florida, it offers luxurious cruise experiences from top cruise lines such as Disney Cruise Line, Royal Caribbean International, and Carnival Cruise Line.
  • Port of Tampa: Roughly a three hour drive from Miami, cruises depart from Port of Tampa for the Caribbean, the Bahamas, and occasional trans-Atlantic trips. Cruise lines include Carnival Cruise Line and Royal Caribbean International.
  • Port of Key West: Located just off the coast of Miami, the historic port of Key West offers weekly cruises to popular destinations such as Cuba, Cozumel, Nassau, Freeport and the Caribbean. Cruise lines include Royal Caribbean International and Carnival Cruise Line.
  • Port of Punta Gorda: Located near the western coast of Florida, cruises depart from Port of Punta Gorda to the Caribbean and include stops in St. Thomas, St. Maarten, and Puerto Rico. Cruise lines like Royal Caribbean International and Majesty Cruises depart from this port.

How can you make the most of your time in Miami before or after your cruise?

  • Enjoy the Art Deco Scenery: Check out South Beach and enjoy the iconic art deco buildings.
  • Take a Boat Ride: Hop on a private charter and sail around Biscayne Bay or Little Havana to enjoy some spectacular views.
  • Immerse Yourself in Cuban Culture: Explore picturesque Little Havana and try your hand at traditional Cuban cuisine.
  • Take a Day Trip to Key West: Take a day-long trip to the island of Key West and explore the colorful culture and artsy atmosphere.
  • Relax at Miami Beach: Spend some time lazing around on the beautiful sandy beaches of South Beach.
  • Go Shopping: Shop till you drop in any of the upscale malls in Miami.
  • Get Adrenaline Rush: Experience the thrill of skydiving at the Miami Skydiving Center.
  • Taste the Local Cuisine: Restaurants all around South Beach offer a variety of delectable dishes, like Cuban sandwiches, empanadas, and mofongo.
  • Take a Walk Through Nature: Walk along one of Miami's many nature trails, like the Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park.
  • Dancing the Night Away: There is no shortage of nightclubs in Miami, so head out, choose any place that suits your taste, and dance all night.
