How to Pack for a Cruise: Tips and Tricks for 2023

Embarking on a cruise is an exciting experience, but the packing process can be overwhelming. Knowing how to pack efficiently for a cruise is essential to ensure that you have everything you need while avoiding overpacking.

In 2023, there are some new tips and tricks that can make the process easier and more efficient. This guide will provide you with all the information you need to pack for a cruise in 2023.

  • Pick Out Your Wardrobe: Choose comfortable, versatile clothing that can easily be layered. Avoid bringing anything overly flashy, as cruise attire is usually relaxed and casual.
  • Pack Clothes for Each Day: When packing, think about the activities and sights you plan on doing while on the cruise and pack accordingly. Throw in a few backups just in case of any unexpected spills or mishaps.
  • Always Carry an Umbrella: Most ships have outdoor decks with plenty of seating and lounging area, but if you plan on taking any shore tours, pack an umbrella in case of inclement weather.
  • Make Sure You Pack Your Necessities: Don't forget your medicine, any special items you may need for medical or dietary needs, toiletries, and especially your wrist watch!
  • Bring a Swimsuit: Even if you don't plan on swimming, you'll definitely want to spend some time soaking in the sunshine or relaxing in the hot tub.
  • Don't Forget the Sunscreen: There's nothing worse than getting sunburned while on vacation. So, slather on the sunscreen to protect your skin!
  • Bring Entertainment: Packing some books, games, or music is always a good idea to pass the time or to enjoy some evening entertainment.
  • Think Light and Compact: To avoid lugging around a bunch of heavy items and dealing with airline baggage fees, try to pack light and opt for collapsible items such as suitcases and headgear.
  • Pack an Extra Charger: As most ships have limited outlets, it's a good idea to be prepared and pack an extra charger so you don't miss out on capturing those special moments.
  • Pack Your ID and Travel Documents: When it comes to international trips, it's mandatory to have your ID, passport, visa, and international driverâ€™s license. Make sure you keep them safe and easily accessible.

How can you maximize your luggage space while still packing everything you need?

  • Lay out everything: you plan to bring on the floor or bed and then pair items together (shirt with pants, sandals with shirt). Pack those pairs together so they take up less space.
  • Pack bulky items: in your check-in bag such as jackets, sweaters, and jackets.
  • Roll up your clothes: This will help you fit more items into your bag. Make sure to place the items in small plastic bags first to keep any sharp edges from poking out.
  • Pack heavier items: at the bottom of the bag and lighter items on the top.
  • Stuff shoes and: other smaller items in the corners of your bag or even in the pockets of your suitcase.
  • Place an inflatable pillow: or a balled up pair of yoga pants inside a personal bath towel. This will help absorb any shocks should your bag get banged up.
  • Place: belts, and other smaller items in an air-tight plastic bag. This will save you a ton of space and will help you avoid having to bring an extra bag.
  • Pack liquids and gels: in a transparent TSA-approved toiletry kit. This way, you don't have to take them out separately while going through the security checkpoint.
  • Line the suitcase: With cushion type items such as clothes. This will create an extra layer of protection if your bag does get banged up or thrown around.
  • Utilize all: the pockets and compartments of the suitcase for storing last-minute items. This will ensure that you're utilizing every inch of open space in your bag.



Are there any packing tips specific to the type of cruise or destination you're traveling to in 2023?

  • Be prepared for: a variety of temperatures and climates! Bring clothes for cool and warm weather, and consider packing a waterproof jacket for sailing and exploring wet and rainy locations.
  • Pack light and smart: pack clothes that you can layer when the temperature changes and that can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion.
  • Don't forget items: to help make your cruise more enjoyable such as books, games, and a life jacket.
  • Remember to bring: a small backpack or tote bag with you to take on shore excursions to store water, snacks, and any souvenirs you may find.
  • Utilize all your space wisely: such as utilizing vacuum-sealed storage bags for bulky clothing items.
  • Don't forget any: medication or personal items you may need throughout the cruise, such as a powerbank, sunblock and bug repellents.
  • Consider packing: a reusable water bottle to be able to fill up whenever you can, as well as reusable shopping bags for souvenirs.
  • Pack an adaptor: for plugs depending on the destination of your cruise.
